CLASSIFIED ASSESSMENT – Apocalypse BREAKER (Book 1)

EYES ONLY
SUBJECT: Dean Williams - Complete Strategic Assessment and Future Projections
FROM: [REDACTED] - Senior Analyst, Department of Extraordinary Threats
TO: Joint Chiefs of Staff

Executive Summary

Since the beginning of System incursions, this department has tracked the emergence and development of enhanced human operatives. Standard progression models suggest that even exceptional individuals require sustained System exposure, extensive training, and favorable circumstances to achieve significant capability enhancement.

Dean Williams represents a fundamental deviation from these models.

Initial Incident - Research Facility C

Subject Williams entered our awareness as a courier delivering classified research components when hostile System forces breached Research Facility C. The nineteen-year-old civilian was subsequently Activated through proximity exposure to a dragon during an attack by a System operative designated "Vek." Williams eliminated the hostile with assistance from a newly unbound dragon entity.

Standard protocols were followed for System Activation processing, though Williams immediately demonstrated possession of latent Mana Sensitivity—an extremely rare trait that dragon exposure had activated.

Enhancement Protocol Authorization

Post-incident analysis revealed Williams' unique genetic predisposition warranted unprecedented intervention. Commander Dennings authorized the dosage of Supplement Alpha for Williams' integration—three full canisters versus the standard 1/50th canister allocation. This decision, equivalent to enhancing 150 standard operatives, was based on scans showing Williams had earned maximum potential System rewards.

The enhancement protocol resulted in all seven primary attributes boosting to 100+ (peak human theoretical maximum: 85).

For context: our most elite operatives typically achieve 60-70 in primary attributes after years of enhancement. Williams achieved 100+ in all categories immediately upon Activation.

In addition to incredible Attributes, once we disabled the disadvantages of Mana Sensitivity he gained access to a 50% boost to any effect that uses mana. And the best part? That’s not his cap. Thanks to the three canisters; his true maximum is 300%. Captain Nicholson, who oversaw his Activation, guessed that implementing too much of his true potential, too quickly, might result in direct System engagement. Thus, she built a plan to gradually unveil his true power.

Tutorial Phase - First Anomaly

Williams completed his tutorial by accessing what our researchers had only theorized existed: "Void Difficulty" quest content. The System's standard progression offers Easy/Moderate/High/Hell difficulty levels. Williams discovered and accessed a hidden tier beyond Hell difficulty.

During this phase, Williams created the custom class "Apocalypse BREAKER," single-handedly eliminated multiple level 10+ Summanati operatives on a frontier world, infiltrated and destroyed an entire System invasion fleet, and acquired three relic-tier magical items including a daemon-corrupted weapon that permanently fused with his hand.

Our monitoring equipment recorded a mana surge of over one million units during his return—readings that should be physically impossible for a level 3 operative.

Initial Assessment - Captain Marvick's Evaluation

Captain Marvick's evaluation revealed capabilities that defied our understanding of System mechanics. Williams demonstrated an ability to partially resist Marvick's Ultimate-class restraint ability—a level 20+ technique that has successfully immobilized our most dangerous hostiles.

Williams was still level 3 at the time.

Medical analysis confirmed Williams' unique trait combination allowed him to generate non-System mana through his "Override Engine" ability, making him a living anomaly that the System both empowers and cannot fully control.

At this point, our models suggested we were dealing with an exceptional but manageable asset.

We were... optimistic.

First Deployment - Thanderkand Treasury Mission

Given Williams' unprecedented capabilities, we assigned Lieutenant Sharon McFay (level 24) as tactical support for what planning classified as a "diplomatic assignment with limited combat exposure." The mission involved preventing two rival adventuring groups from looting an ancient treasury.

Within 48 hours, Williams (with Sharon’s help) had advanced from level 6 to level 10 and eliminated threats with combined levels exceeding 80. His approach to "diplomacy" included single-handedly defeating a level 25 Elite takaltu commander and eliminating a Captain Jasteel Burstmeister in aerial combat.

The Daemon Incident

During this deployment, Williams successfully performed what our xenobiology department classifies as a "moderately suicidal" summoning ritual. HUD medical data confirms his heart stopped for over a minute during the process.

Lieutenant McFay performed emergency resuscitation, preventing permanent death. The result was Williams binding a daemon entity he has named "Robin"—a creature resembling what he describes as "a small dinosaur with the disposition of an enthusiastic house cat."

When asked about the wisdom of befriending a daemon, Williams replied: "He's a good boy. He only eats the bad guys."

Mission Escalation

The team successfully infiltrated ancient necromantic facilities, acquired pre-System magical knowledge, and established intelligence contacts with extra-dimensional entities. Williams demonstrated successful casting of spells he had learned minutes earlier, including Possess Interior, which caused a Greater Lich to tear itself apart from the inside.

His combat log notes read: "Worked better than expected. Very gross. Would use again."

Aside: During this mission, Lieutenant McFay was wounded with a life-threatening curse. Both she and Dean have proven reluctant to elaborate on the precise details.

Surface Engagement Analysis

Upon emerging from the catacombs, Williams encountered a three-way battle between elite clovash assassins, a diseased okaku barbarian, and an entity our xenobiology department believes was Telkazorin himself operating through a possessed shell.

Rather than retreat, Williams chose to intervene against a Level 18 Elite Clovash Assassin while carrying his wounded analyst. Combat data shows him surviving instantly fatal wounds and defeating an opponent with four times his combat experience.

The Champion Incident

Gentlemen, what I must report next has been verified through multiple independent sources, yet challenges every mathematical model we possess.

Williams encountered a System Champion—a wyvern variant enhanced with Unbreakable, Spawning, and Magekiller modifications. Rather than retreat, he engaged it in single combat.

He won.

His method, according to combat logs, involved crawling inside the creature's eye socket and manually destroying its brain with his relic weapon. When asked about his tactical reasoning, Williams explained: "The outside was too hard, so I went for the squishy bits. They didn’t taste very good."

Our combat analysis department confirms the statistical probability of this victory approaches zero.

Current Capability Assessment

Williams has returned from deployment having completed objectives we classified as theoretically impossible. His demonstrated capabilities now include:

·         Combat effectiveness against opponents 10+ levels above his classification

·         Successful casting of pre-System magic with minimal training

·         Natural resistance to mental influence and System manipulation

·         Formation of alliances with entities existing outside normal space-time

·         Mana readings that exceed our instruments' measurement capabilities

His power progression follows no known curve. Each engagement results in exponential capability increase rather than incremental development.

System Response

The controlling intelligence has initiated an "Event Burst" in direct response to Williams' activities, accelerating mandatory System deployment timelines by 40-95% with dramatically increased difficulty scaling.

This suggests the System either considers Williams a significant threat requiring accelerated response, or views him as an opportunity for enhanced challenge development.

We can no longer determine which interpretation is correct.

Psychological Evaluation

Williams entered the program as a borderline sociopath. Activation appears to have granted him an ability to empathize; effectively “waking him up” from a narrow emotional spectrum.

According to Lieutenant McFay, the man has a compulsion to protect children and those he deems his friends. He appears to take his goal of stopping apocalypses (whatever that means) seriously. Outside of those parameters, she warns that he has retained his sociopathic tendencies.

To say that he has issues with authority is an understatement.

Strategic Implications

Williams represents either humanity's ultimate strategic asset or our most significant containment challenge. Current trajectory suggests his power growth will continue beyond our ability to measure, predict, or potentially control.

The System's accelerated response indicates Williams' activities have fundamentally altered the cosmic conflict's parameters. We are no longer operating within established frameworks.

Final Assessment

Since the beginning of System incursions, I have analyzed hundreds of enhanced operatives. None approach Williams' trajectory. He doesn't just exceed our models—he suggests entirely new possibilities for human development under System influence.

Williams has begun demonstrating capabilities our models suggest should be impossible: Elder Insights typically restricted to transcendent entities, relic corruption effects that modify System items on contact, and a daemon familiar capable of evolutionary enhancement through enemy consumption.

Whether this represents humanity's salvation or extinction depends entirely on maintaining Williams' fundamental humanity while his capabilities transcend human limitations.

Recommendations

Based on comprehensive analysis, Williams will likely continue exponential development regardless of our intervention. We recommend maximum organizational support with prepared contingencies for scenarios beyond our current understanding.

Most critically: Williams appears to genuinely care about protecting others. This may be our only reliable constraint on his development.

We are in uncharted territory with planetary stakes.

Prelude: Dangerous Negotiations

System Zone: 15121B

Adventurer Presence: Azagrand Crusade Deployment Fleet

"What have you brought us, Squire Teluum?" Fleet Commander Kryst's magically modulated voice filled the space, echoing off yellow, exposed bones. Standing atop a dais carved directly into the hollowed-out remains of some asteroid-sized monster, the man lifted an armored hand. His spike-tipped fingers curled, urging the squire forward.

Security Legates, each dressed in heavily enchanted full plate armor and bearing weapons exposed, rotated to follow the squire’s approach. Behind closed visors, a dozen sets of eyes fixed on the creature following the low-ranking officer.

The guest was an anomaly. Among the Crusade, all wore full suits of plate armor. Even the squire's relatively slight body was concealed behind magically sealed steel. This newcomer wore a robe of shimmering red and orange, its pale, doll-like body featureless save for the overlapping scales that began between its eyes and flowed upward across its otherwise bald head.

Moving with smooth, boneless precision, the creature followed the squire quietly, looking neither left nor right. If being surrounded by twelve beings outfitted entirely in relics bothered it, the outsider showed no reaction.

Teluum drew to a halt ten feet from the base of the commander’s bone platform. Drawing his curled fist against his left breast, he spoke in a light, youthful voice. "Commander, this one was discovered by one of the scouts. It was piloting a vessel of unfamiliar design, resistant to analysis. This... thing calls itself 'Voice' and says it wishes to negotiate."

The Commander studied Voice thoughtfully, helm flashing with subtle magics as he used the System to activate various spells. He spoke with wry curiosity, "Was it assessed?"

"It was," Teluum replied quickly. "They have determined the creature is some sort of augmented bio-construct of old magic design. It is not Activated."

"You are called Voice?" Kryst asked the creature, waving a hand to indicate the guest should speak.

"We are." The pale, snake-like thing had a warm, melodious voice entirely at odds with its features. It flowed into a bow, arms spread wide, head tilted down in submission. "Voice has been sent to negotiate. The desire is to trade precious information in exchange for trinkets."

"It is unusual for those who have not converted to entreat with us. It is even more unusual for the Crusade to negotiate at all." The Commander sounded amused, although some undercurrent in the delivery sent a wave of tension through the nearby soldiers.

Straightening, Voice made a waving gesture. Its hand was as pale and flawless as cream, save for the silver claws that tipped each digit. "You are known to us, Commander Kryst. Seventy-five Events prior, you entreated with the one whom I serve. He assisted you in defeating a rival in exchange for a boon."

Kryst inhaled sharply. "Telkazorin. You are his." It wasn't a question.

"We are." Voice proudly agreed. "And through his greatness, you gained what the System calls a Conquest, did you not?"

The earlier tension traveled to the Commander, stripping all previous warmth from his tone. "Our agreement was for concluded business, not future arrangements."

"Perhaps it is time for new business, Commander." Voice turned, its pale eyes sliding across the chamber.

Kryst's command bridge was large, nearly a hundred feet across. Control mechanisms jutted from various points in the tendon-covered walls, armored Experts manning them. There were no view ports, no screens with which to observe the stellar space the vessel occupied. Those with connections to the System, and the HUD provided by it, had no need for such things.

If there had been such a view port, it would have revealed the crowded vastness of System space, a realm thick with migrated stars. Commander Kryst's fleet, nearly three hundred ships strong, would have orbited the flagship like planetoids, each laden with soldiers and servants.

Voice paused, studying nothing, penetrating gaze appearing to take in the assemblage. "Your ranks have swollen since our master saw you last. We are most impressed by your climb to power. It is a shame that your rival commanders continue to outshine you in the eyes of the System, is it not?"

At that, grips tightened audibly nearby. It was enough to cause Kryst to glance at his HUD, issuing silent orders to his warriors. They settled with reluctance.

"Speak your purpose, creation of the Dragon Mage," the Commander demanded.

Voice moved its attention to the tunnels lining either side of the chamber. Carved out of the corpse's bone and meat, each was secured with solid metal plating covered with inscriptions. It spoke thoughtfully, offhandedly, "We have information about your scout. Veknel was his name, we are told."

Its emphasis on the past tense was unmistakable.

A Legates, one still gripping an oversized sword too tightly, straightened. It was a subtle gesture, one nearly impossible to detect. Voice's lipless mouth twitched, betraying the creature's ability to discern the micro expression.

Behind his visored concealment the Commander's eyes narrowed. "An Expansion Monitor sent Veknel to a pre-acquisition frontier planet. We have every reason to believe he may be gone for months, perhaps years. Does your master have information otherwise?"

"He would not risk one of his creations without reason." Voice returned its attention to Kryst, the overlapping scales decorating its head briefly reflecting the ambient light with an oily shimmer. "You will provide us with two tons of post-refinement Containment Metal. Furthermore, you will allow us to accompany your fleet on any retributive engagements made. In exchange, we will provide you with guidance to the location of those who have despoiled Veknel."

Inhaling, Kryst was about to speak when Voice lifted a hand, pressing on. "There are two others, each from rival fleets, who have faced a similar fate. Your ways are strange to us, but we understand there is a code of sorts, yes? That avenging them would grant honor to your fleet and reduce the standing of your rivals?"

Of all the offers made, the last was enough to make the Commander hesitate. "Containment Metal is not exactly a cheap commodity. Risking the System's ire to assault a pre-acquisition could bring ruin to more than my fleet."

With an amused glint in its eyes, Voice retorted, "Then you will need to raise funds and move with care. Our presence with your fleet will help with the second. Consider it a good faith gesture. As for the first, we believe you know how to grow capital and sharpen the edge of your invading force simultaneously."

"You're suggesting I host a Jubilee."

"We are."

"That would require I spend the very Conquest your master helped me obtain. Do you comprehend the value of such a boon?"

Voice studied Kryst silently for several seconds before replying, "And the opportunity I offer is not worth such a thing?"

"Perhaps. Return to your vessel. I will send word when I've made my decision."

"As you command." With another flourishing bow, Voice turned and followed the squire from the room to the teleportation chamber.

* * *

Voice's ship was a marvel of old magic composition. Resembling a floating house covered in scales, a pair of clawed feet folded beneath the main body. It floated in the vacuum of space confidently, openly defiant of its constant state of conflict.

A corona glowed around the vessel, stretching for miles in every direction. The System's passive effort to infiltrate and assimilate the old magic pulsed and throbbed violently, creating a light show bright enough to be mistaken for a newborn star. For all it was akin to a submarine in the deepest of trenches, the house showed no signs of faltering.

Appearing inside a teleportation circle, Voice's body briefly shimmered as cleansing spells removed the System's taint. An animated creation with a human skull atop a spider's body spoke from nearby, its voice tinny and smooth.

"You have been purified, Voice."

With a dismissive gesture, Voice strode from the chamber and into the house proper. Other than a few other constructs similar to the spider, the interior was mundane. Among standard humanoid races, it might have been considered well-appointed, if rustic.

Wallpaper covered the walls, and the floors were decorated with spell-woven rugs thick enough to muffle sound. Ornamentation, from pictures to handcrafted furniture, artfully defined libraries, private studies, experimentation chambers, and much more.

Passing through it all, Voice came to a stop at a bare wall. A gesture and whispered spell removed the illusion concealing the door and granted it access. Inside was a room lined with pulsing, throbbing tissue. In its center was a diamond-carved spell circle with a velvet cushion at its center.

Although the ritual carvings would have been immune to anything short of a direct assault, Voice still moved gingerly across them. Taking up a position in the center of the spell, it knelt, hands folding before its torso as if in supplication.

"Great Master, I have delivered your offer."

A ripple of motion preceded a sense of presence flooding the room. Telkazorin's raw will, unleashed and focused, was akin to gravity: it changed the very physics of space and time around it.

"Did he decode your true purpose?" The speaker's voice was rough, raw with anger.

Voice smiled in earnest at the question. "Master, the System's hold blinds even those it holds in high esteem. Commander Kryst is an intelligent man, but once we baited the hook, his heart rate nearly doubled. We could smell his desire, and that of those in his thrall. There is little room for caution or sense in them."

"Be careful, my Voice. Do not mistake an abundance of ambition for a lack of cunning." Telkazorin's rebuke was gentle, almost paternal.

"As you say, Master. If I may?"

"Go ahead."

Phrasing the question carefully, Voice said, "It is a matter of orchestration. If we receive the refined Containment Metal, will we have sufficient time to prepare the infusions, collect them, and then locate and secure the vessel? Should the Crusade move quickly, it could undermine our efforts."

"It is an excellent question, and a valid concern both. But I would not worry. The Jubilee will provide many opportunities." The Dragon Mage paused for several seconds before adding, "Although... there is one small concern in that direction. One I did not recognize until nearly too late."

"Master?"

"If my future vessel joins the Jubilee, he may inadvertently alter our plans. His premature death would be most... irksome. I will take additional steps to ensure that does not happen."

Voice's smile wavered. "Is circumspection the best choice? Should he arrive, perhaps we could collect him. Our life would be but a small sacrifice to achieve your fruition."

"If such an opportunity arises, you have my permission to make the sacrifice. Do so only if you are certain of success. Otherwise, remain in the shadows. The Jubilee will not be safe, not with whatever methods the vessel has used to blind the System to his true nature. Should one of its Monitors take note of our machinations, it could result in an unacceptable loss of resources."

Head bowed in acquiescence, Voice murmured, "We live in service. Your will, our command."

The pressure in the room eased as Telkazorin's spell came to an end. Soul-deep weariness stole into Voice's bones at its master's absence. Much of the mana required to fuel the communications spell came from within it; given the location, such a simple spell had cost it an ocean of magic.

Climbing weakly to its feet, Voice staggered from the room.

Chapter 1: Breakfast of Champions

Time Since Last Event: 12 Days

"That's enough warm up," Marvick shouted, his baritone voice carrying through the practice room. "Let's make Trip earn his breakfast."

Fighting a tight smile, I spun in time to deflect a spinning kick from Tunde. Moving with speed more suited to anime, he flowed with my defense, landing on one foot, pivoting, and driving a side kick into my sternum.

I inhaled, tightening my core so the blow wouldn’t take my breath away. It still stung and sent me stumbling a few feet.

A whisper of motion was all the warning I had before Lieutenant Chantal Moreau joined the match. Wielding a blunted dagger made of black metal, I barely had time to register her proximity before she was jabbing me with the thing. Blunt or not, the woman's strength and skill combined to make the thrusts more than a little painful.

Moving as she and Tunde had been teaching me, I brought both arms up. Rotating them in circular, rhythmic motions, I guided several strikes aside causing them to miss while blunting others. For several seconds, it felt routine, with the Lieutenant actively helping me push my defenses.

"Come on, Easy-A!" Tunde called, his taunting interrupting the exchange. The nickname had started as a joke about my "easy" Activation—three full canisters of the upgraded serum rather than the standard dose, which was just a few drops from one. After a week and a half of training with the assassination specialist, I'd learned he only made noise as a distraction.

Rotating my hips, I lunged at Chantal, reaching for her tactical vest. The maneuver left me open for a jab to the sternum. I accepted the punishment as a fair price for success when my fingers closed on her armor.

Her expression immediately darkened. "I hate this part," she said, French accent thick.

Chuckling, I whirled, effortlessly lifting her from her feet and using her body as a weapon. Her feet swung around, knocking Tunde's next attack off course. I released her at the moment of collision and both went down in a tangle of limbs.

Arms wrapped around my torso as Captain Marvick joined the melee. If anyone at Counterstrike was nearly as strong as I was, it was the captain. The man's bulging muscles had more in common with steel pylons than flesh. He was an inch or two taller than I was, too, which gave him several advantages. Hands locked around me, he held me in place long enough for yet another member of the Scorched Mobility Battalion to get in on the fun.

Wiz, the team's spellcasting specialist, adjusted his training rifle and fired from thirty feet away. The short young man was just as capable with the gun as he was with magic. Paintball pellets stitched down my stomach as he added insult to the pile-on.

"Hey," I called as one of the shots rebounded off the protective armor between my thighs.

"Oops! Sorry!" Wiz replied loudly. He meant it, too. We'd learned that things tended to go downhill fast if I got too angry.

"All's fair in war," Marvick said at my back, clearly amused.

"Oh, yeah?" Without waiting for a reply, I slammed my head backward, striking the man's chin. The audacious maneuver earned me two things. First, it sent lines of fire through my skull, triggering an instant headache. Second, it made him grunt and slacken his hold.

Using the opportunity, I brought both of my hands to his wrists, forcing them apart. For a tense second, I thought I was going to win.

Then, with a chuckle and a growled, "Almost," in my ear, he resecured his hold. My ribs groaned their displeasure and breathing became difficult. The HUD flashing in the upper corner of my vision offered a solution, although I resisted the temptation.

I'd enabled the Strength limiter for a reason.

Both Chantal and Tunde came into view, each taking a flanking position. Although Chantal wasn't officially a member of SMB, she moved with the same lethal poise, her knees bent and weight low.

As Marvick adjusted his hold, twisting me to give my opponents a better killing angle, I caught sight of the final two members of the local SMB unit. Cruz and Texas were leaning against the far wall, both clearly enjoying the spectacle.

"You go, Trip!" Texas drawled, the lanky man's eyes bright with mockery.

I made a mental note to break one of his fingers in a future spar.

"Ready to call it?" Marvick asked, shaking me from side to side as he lifted me far enough for my relic boots to no longer grant me purchase. Their levitation effect was reduced even in the mana-intensification zone of the training room, meaning he only had to add an inch or so before I lost all traction.

I looked around, gauging my options. Chantal was just a few feet away, and Tunde had started that tongue-clicking thing of his. The mahogany-skinned man with his tiger stripe-scarred cheeks had dropped all pretense at play.

If I didn't call it, he'd go all-out.

SMB went hard during our training sessions. Being able to recover from a broken bone in a matter of hours took off almost all limitations, allowing us to push beyond anything resembling human limits.

I hesitated, running through the scenarios as fast as my heightened Mind and Perception Attributes allowed. There were ways I could free myself, but most of them were what I'd have considered cheating. Unlocking my full Strength or deliberately putting myself in the mindset to trigger my class would undermine the purpose of this training. Our objective was to help me up my Organic Skills and to gain more practical combat experience, not remind everyone how lethal I could be with my abilities fully unleashed.

Marvick tightened his grip as I assessed my options. Various scenes from action movies played through my imagination. The problem wasn't that reenacting those sequences was impossible; with my Grace and Strength, I could do a lot. No, the issue was that I hadn't learned enough to execute any of them properly.

Raw Ability scores only got me so far, as the team had taught me time and time again.

"Are we calling it?" Marvick pressed as Chantal came within striking range.

"Call it," a new voice announced as Lieutenant Sharon McFay came striding into the facility. The petite, white-haired analyst waved. I couldn't tell if it was at me or the captain, so I went with the more likely answer and waved back.

She gave me a flat look. "Dean, what are you doing? You’re being hugged to death by a tree. Why would I wave at you?"

Resisting the urge to make a comment about wudgies, I just grinned. "Because Marvick knows if he let go long enough to wave back, I'd knock him on his ass."

It wasn't precisely true, but neither was it impossible. I'd reduced my Strength down to a hundred, mostly to avoid accidentally breaking people. Even that was enough to do damage.

The captain responded to my boast by tightening his grip. I felt a rib creak, probably on the edge of fracturing.

Chantal laughed at my expression. It was a light, friendly sound that was entirely ruined when she flourished her dagger in my face. "I'm happy to stab you a few more times, if it helps."

I twisted my neck far enough to catch Marvick's eye and rasped. "Sharon wouldn't interrupt without good reason, right?"
