LitRPGTools.com has shipped one of the features its long-term users have been waiting for: native Patreon integration. Readers who support the platform on Patreon can now link their account, get verified, and receive visible patron recognition directly on the site. Authors can go further — a full OAuth connection ties their Patreon data into their platform analytics.

Here's what it does, how to use it, and why it's a meaningful step for how genre databases are sustained.

## What Changed

The integration went live as part of a recent platform update. The new Patreon section lives in your LitRPGTools.com Settings page, alongside links for Amazon, Goodreads, and social accounts.

There are two distinct paths:

**For readers (patron linking)**: Enter the email address associated with your Patreon account. An admin verifies your active patron status and confirms your tier. Once verified, your LitRPGTools.com profile displays a patron badge with your tier. You're recognized as a platform supporter visibly and permanently.

**For authors (OAuth connection)**: Authors get a full Patreon OAuth integration — a direct, two-way connection between their Patreon account and their LitRPGTools.com author dashboard. This feeds into the platform's author analytics intelligence layer, which tracks how Patreon-connected readers engage with an author's catalog on LitRPGTools.com.

Both paths are now live. The reader path is available to anyone in Settings. The author path is accessible through the Author Dashboard.

## Why This Matters for How Genre Databases Work

LitRPGTools.com is not a corporate product. It's a genre-specific tool built by and for the LitRPG community — and it has to be paid for somehow.

The problem with voluntary donation models is that they're invisible. You can Patreon-support a platform and have no presence inside it — no signal to yourself that it's doing anything, no recognition from others that you're contributing. The integration fixes that asymmetry. Patron support becomes visible: on your profile, in the platform's understanding of who its core users are, and in the tier-matched benefits you receive.

Based on our analysis of the LitRPG community's relationship with platforms like this, reader investment tends to track directly with feature velocity. Platforms with stronger patron communities ship faster because they can justify the development time. The catalog maintenance, slop detection, reading order tools, and community ratings that make LitRPGTools.com useful don't happen for free.

The Patreon integration connects the people most invested in seeing those features improve with the platform's ability to build them.

## The Author Side: Analytics Intelligence

The author Patreon OAuth is worth examining separately.

When an author connects their Patreon to LitRPGTools.com, the platform gains visibility into which Patreon-supporting readers are also active catalog users. That data — what those readers are tracking, what ratings they're leaving, which titles they're completing — is the kind of audience intelligence that matters for an author understanding their reader base.

More specifically: a reader who pays $10/month on Patreon AND has a LitRPGTools.com library with 40+ tracked books AND consistently rates new releases is a different signal than anonymous catalog traffic. The author analytics intelligence layer can identify and surface those patterns.

This isn't about exposing individual reader data. It's about helping authors understand whether their Patreon community maps to their LitRPGTools.com reader community — and whether the books they're writing are reaching the readers who already care enough to pay for more.

## How to Get Started

If you support LitRPGTools.com on Patreon and haven't linked your account yet:

1. Go to [LitRPGTools.com](https://litrpgtools.com) and log in
2. Navigate to Settings
3. Find the Patreon section
4. Enter your Patreon-linked email and submit
5. Wait for admin verification (usually within 24-48 hours)

Once verified, your patron badge shows on your profile and your benefits activate.

If you're a LitRPGTools.com reader who isn't currently a patron but has been thinking about it: this is the moment where supporting the platform has visible, permanent presence. The catalog you're using to find LitRPG books — the one with [50,000+ tracked titles](/lists/best-litrpg-books), community ratings, reading orders, and now active AI slop detection — stays useful because readers like you keep it running.

The genre's best discovery tool shouldn't have to rely on goodwill alone. Now it doesn't have to.
